About Me

Personal Introduction

Hi everyone, my name is Yahya Ara. I am 18 and I study Computer Engineering at Istanbul Technical University.

Academic Interests

I have two primary interests: Computers and Math.

My interest in computers dates back to my chilhood and is mostly a result of my interest in video games. When I was around 4-5 years old I started playing computer games with my fellow pre-school friends. These were mostly online games from websites like Friv or Oyun Skor. Then, with the occasion of my father, I started playing moreadvanced games like Age of Empires III. After a while, around middle school, I had the perfect idea that I could create my own video game. So I looked into game dev for children I found Scratch by MIT. I immediately got hooked to it and created all sorts of games and animations using the tools I was given. However, around 7th grade I realised that unfortunately Scratch was insufficient for my dreams to come true so I decided to learn something more eligible for actual video game development where I settled on Unity. I learned C# and followed tutorials made by the legendary YouTuber Brackeys. Then the pandemic happened which caused a major setback in my game dev learning progress because instead of learning to make video games and being productive I just sat at home and played Valorant which triggered my eventual disillusionment with video games. Nowadays, although I haven't dropped playing video games I mostly play games with my friends as a means of socialistion. Nonetheless, my interest in computers outweighted my interest in gaming and I am still very much interested in software development in general (not necessarily game dev).

My interest in math however is much more recent. During primary education I mostly viewed math as a subject and didn't think much of it. In high school because I didn't study and mostly played games I got low marks from math so I thought I was quite bad at math and started to hate it slightly. However around 12th grade I had an academic epiphany and realised that I was thinking about math wrong this whole time. Beneath the fact that math was a school, a whole lot of interesting phenomena was hidden. I started appreciate the abstract nature and the certainty associated with mathematics. I am currently undecided about what I exactly should do with this interest. I might pursue academic math in the future but for the time being I think I will just keep it as a hobby.
